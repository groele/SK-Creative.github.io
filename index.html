Research Positioning

My work targets programmable optical behavior in atomically thin heterostructures. I focus on how charge transfer, interfacial polarization, and crystallographic alignment reshape excitonic response and valley selectivity.

This line of research combines materials synthesis, low-temperature spectroscopy, and data-driven interpretation to deliver physically grounded routes toward memory-like and logic-like photonic functions.

Primary Focus Areas

  • Interfacial exciton regulation in van der Waals stacks
  • Gate and ferroelectric control of exciton-trion conversion
  • Polarization-selective spectroscopy under cryogenic conditions
  • Research software for scientific figure and presentation workflows
